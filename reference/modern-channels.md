# Modern Channels — what's actually moving in 2025–2026

Skills load this so they treat current channels as first-class, not as bolt-ons to a 2015 mental model.

## The channel landscape, current

### Platforms reshaping marketing right now

| Channel | What changed | Why marketers care |
|---|---|---|
| **TikTok-native creative** | Vertical, sub-15-sec, sound-on, hook-first storytelling. "TikTok-made me-buy-it" loop. | Direct revenue attribution via TikTok Shop in many markets. Highest watch-completion rates of any platform. |
| **Reels & Shorts** | Meta + YouTube cloned the format, but algos reward different signals (saves, sends on Reels; watch time on Shorts) | Reach play for brands locked out of TikTok (US gov context, brand-safety policies) |
| **Creator economy maturity** | Shift from one-off sponsorships → equity stakes, co-owned brands, exclusive product lines | Mr Beast × Feastables. Logan Paul × Prime. Emma Chamberlain × Chamberlain Coffee. |
| **Retail Media Networks (RMNs)** | Amazon Ads, Walmart Connect, Target Roundel, Instacart, Carrefour Links — closed-loop attribution from ad to purchase | The fastest-growing ad channel since 2022. Eating into Meta/Google share. |
| **Connected TV (CTV)** | Streaming inventory now bigger than linear in US/UK. Netflix, Disney+, Prime Video all sell ads. | Reach play with TV-quality attention; addressability that linear lacked. |
| **Audio (podcast + smart-speaker)** | Podcast ad revenue passing $2B annually; Spotify Ad Studio democratizing entry | Highest-trust ad medium per Edelman. Long-form listenership = brand-build, not just DR. |
| **Substack & creator newsletters** | Direct subscription = no algo intermediation. Brands sponsoring or building owned newsletters. | High-intent, premium audience. Distribution moat. |
| **Reddit (post-IPO)** | Reddit Ads Pro launched, AMA-as-marketing, brand subreddits | Long-tail intent + community. Brand-safety improving. |
| **Discord / Telegram brand communities** | Owned community as retention + UGC channel | Web3 / gaming / creator brands lead; spreading to lifestyle |
| **In-game advertising (real, finally)** | Roblox brand experiences; Fortnite Creative; Anzu/Frameplay programmatic in-game | Reach Gen Alpha + Z without traditional formats |
| **Generative-AI native creative** | Brands shipping 100s–1000s of asset variants from one master concept; AI-localized creative; AI agent–to–agent commerce surfacing | Unit economics of personalized creative inverted. New ethics issues. |
| **AI agents as a channel (emerging)** | When buyers use AI agents for research/purchase, brand discoverability shifts to "what does the agent see + recommend" | Not yet major budget but watch closely. SEO is gradually becoming AEO (Agent Engine Optimization). |

### Channels in steady decline (relatively)

- Linear TV (especially in 18–34 markets)
- Print magazines (a few luxury exceptions)
- Display banners (<0.1% CTR average)
- Twitter/X organic for B2C brands (paid still works for some segments)
- Generic email blasts (segmented + behavioral email is healthy; spray-and-pray is dying)
- Long-form blog SEO for topics LLMs answer directly

## Format conventions worth knowing

### TikTok / Reels / Shorts
- Hook in first 1.5 seconds (faces, motion, on-screen text + sound match)
- Native audio always (use trending sounds when fit; "sound on" is the default)
- "Hook escalation" — add a second hook at 3s, third at 7s
- Captions burnt-in (most viewers watch with no sound on Reels)

### CTV
- 15s and 30s spots dominate; 6s for awareness
- Premium = co-viewing context (people watch CTV with others)
- Frequency capping is critical — same spot 8x in one episode kills brand favorability

### Podcast
- Host-read endorsements outperform produced ads ~2-3x in brand lift
- 30-60s mid-roll = sweet spot for brand-build; 15s pre-roll for DR
- Dynamic insertion now table-stakes; baked-in only for top-tier sponsorships

### Retail Media
- Sponsored Products on Amazon = bottom-funnel conversion play
- Sponsored Brands + Display = upper-funnel within the retailer
- Off-site retailer-data audiences = the new lookalike

## "Modern" judging criteria emerging in awards

When deep-diving 2024–2026 work, these are the lenses awards juries (Cannes Innovation, WARC Effective, Effie) increasingly apply:

1. **Earned-to-paid ratio** — what % of total impressions came earned?
2. **Cultural hijack** — did the brand insert into a moment it didn't own?
3. **Modular creative** — was the creative system designed to scale across markets/formats?
4. **First-party data activation** — was the campaign built on data the brand owned (loyalty, app, subscription)?
5. **AI augmentation** — was AI used for personalization, scaling, optimization (and disclosed when needed)?
6. **Attention quality** — attention seconds, not impressions
7. **Long-term brand contribution** — did the campaign build/use a Distinctive Brand Asset?
8. **Effectiveness measurability** — was the methodology MMM-validated, brand-lift-tested, or experiment-designed?

## What "modern" does NOT mean

Modern ≠ new-for-its-own-sake. The most awarded "modern" 2025–2026 campaigns include direct-mail (yes, physical mail), OOH stunts, and packaging changes. The medium isn't what makes it modern — the **mechanic** does.

Examples of "old medium, modern mechanic" that won big at recent shows:
- Outdoor billboards used as live data displays
- Print ads as QR-launched AR experiences
- Direct-mail product drops engineered for unboxing video sharing
- Radio spots dynamically generated per listener
