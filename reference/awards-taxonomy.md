## How to weigh awards

A campaign that won **Grand Prix at Cannes** + **Grand Effie** + cracked **WARC Effective 100** = uncontested elite. Single-tier-2 wins are signal but not sufficient on their own — pair with effectiveness proof or cultural breakthrough evidence.

## Watch-outs

1. **Scam ads** — campaigns created mostly to win awards rather than to run as real client work. Cannes Lions has investigated and rescinded awards historically (notable cases involving Brazilian agencies). Always cross-check against the brand's actual paid-media presence and trade press flagging. Recent example: Cannes Lions opened investigations into select DDB DM9 entries; reflect that context in any analysis.

2. **Self-awarded "industry" awards** — be skeptical of awards bodies that are vendor-funded ("the Best Use of [Vendor's Tech] Award") unless they're genuinely earned in a competitive judged process.

3. **Pay-to-enter ≠ judged-on-merit.** Most legit awards have entry fees (judging costs money) — entry fee alone is not a red flag. Red flag is when **everyone who pays wins something**.

## Effectiveness reference works

When citing effectiveness, lean on these published, peer-respected works:

- **Les Binet & Peter Field — *The Long and the Short of It* (IPA, 2013)** — established the 60/40 brand/activation rule
- **Byron Sharp — *How Brands Grow* (Ehrenberg-Bass, 2010)** — physical & mental availability, light-buyer focus
- **Karen Nelson-Field — *The Attention Economy and How Media Works* (2020)** — attention as currency
- **Mark Ritson — Marketing Week column + Mini MBA** — practitioner critic-in-chief
- **Effie Index** — annual ranking of most effective brands/agencies
