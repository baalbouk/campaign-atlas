# Source List — where to look, ranked by trust

Skills load this so they fetch from the right places, in the right order.

## Tier 1 — primary sources (highest trust)

When a campaign is mentioned here, treat it as fact.

### Award bodies (winner archives)
- **Cannes Lions** — canneslions.com/awards/winners
- **Effie** — effie.org/case_studies (Effie Index + case database)
- **WARC** — warc.com/rankings (Creative 100, Effective 100, plus WARC Awards database — paywall on full case PDFs but public summaries/winner lists are free)
- **D&AD** — dandad.org/awards/professional/winners
- **One Show** — oneshow.org/winners
- **Clio** — clios.com/awards/winners
- **ADC** — adcglobal.org/award-winners
- **Webby** — winners.webbyawards.com
- **Spikes Asia** — spikes.asia/winners
- **Dubai Lynx** — dubailynx.com/winners
- **Eurobest** — eurobest.com/winners
- **AdFest** — adfest.com/results
- **El Sol** — elsolfestival.com (winners archive)
- **El Ojo de Iberoamérica** — elojodeiberoamerica.com (winners)
- **NY Festivals** — newyorkfestivals.com/winners
- **Cresta** — cresta-awards.com/winners
- **EPICA** — epica-awards.com/winners
- **Lürzer's Archive** — lurzersarchive.com (curatorial; treat as Tier 1.5 — editorial taste, not award)

### Brand-side primary
- Brand newsroom / press pages
- Brand investor relations (annual reports often quantify campaign impact for blue-chips)
- Official brand YouTube/Vimeo (the actual creative)

### Effectiveness research
- IPA Effectiveness Awards — ipa.co.uk/effectivenessawards
- IPA Databank — ipa.co.uk/effectiveness-databank (membership)
- Ehrenberg-Bass Institute publications — marketingscience.info
- Ariyh research summaries — ariyh.com (peer-reviewed marketing research → 3-min reads)

## Tier 4 — secondary aggregators (use with care)

These compile lists but aren't authoritative. Use as starting points, then verify in Tier 1–2.

- "Top 10 ads of [year]" listicles (most marketing magazines do these)
- Reddit r/advertising, r/marketing
- LinkedIn posts (case-by-case — practitioner experts okay; lead-gen content shaky)
- Vimeo / YouTube curator channels (Ads of the World, Creative Bloq)
- ScamAds-type watchdog accounts on social

## Avoid

- SEO content farms with "Top 100 Best Marketing Campaigns of All Time"–style listicles unless from Tier 2 publications
- AI-generated marketing blog posts (often hallucinate campaigns)
- Vendor-sponsored "research" presented as independent
- Marketing-newsletter sponsored "case studies" (usually paid placements)

## Search-query patterns that work

For finding award winners:
- `[award name] [year] winners`
- `site:canneslions.com [brand or campaign keyword]`
- `[campaign name] case study site:warc.com`

For finding effectiveness data:
- `[campaign name] sales lift`
- `[campaign name] effectiveness IPA`
- `[brand] annual report [year] [campaign]`

For finding controversy / scam-ad flags:
- `[campaign name] scam ad`
- `[campaign name] investigation`
- `[campaign name] withdrawn award`

For region-specific:
- `[campaign name] [country] case study`
- Use the regional trade press as the search target
